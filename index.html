1984 is a dystopian novel about a society controlled through surveillance, propaganda, and the manipulation of language. It follows Winston Smith, who begins to question the “official truth” in a world where independent thought is dangerous.

Fragment link: read a short overview here .

What makes the book memorable is how it shows power working not only through force, but also through controlling information and rewriting reality. Even today, it’s often used to discuss privacy, media manipulation, and political fear.
